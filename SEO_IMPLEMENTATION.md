# SEO Implementation Guide

This document outlines the SEO improvements implemented for the WeDesignz platform.

## ‚úÖ Implemented Features

### 1. Enhanced Metadata
- **Root Layout** (`app/layout.tsx`): Comprehensive metadata including:
  - Title templates with default and dynamic titles
  - Rich descriptions with keywords
  - Open Graph tags for social media sharing
  - Twitter Card metadata
  - Robots directives for search engines
  - Canonical URLs

### 2. Structured Data (JSON-LD)
- **Organization Schema**: Includes logo, contact information, and business details
  - This is crucial for Google to display your logo in search results
  - Located in `components/SEO/StructuredData.tsx`
- **Website Schema**: Search functionality and site information
- **Breadcrumb Schema**: Available for use on pages with navigation paths

### 3. Robots.txt
- Created `app/robots.ts` to control search engine crawling
- Allows public pages, blocks private areas (dashboard, auth, etc.)
- Points to sitemap location

### 4. Sitemap.xml
- Created `app/sitemap.ts` with all public pages
- Includes priority and change frequency for each page
- Automatically generated by Next.js

### 5. Page-Specific Metadata
All public pages now include:
- Unique titles and descriptions
- Canonical URLs
- Open Graph tags
- Twitter Card metadata
- Relevant keywords

Updated pages:
- `/` (Homepage)
- `/about`
- `/contact`
- `/blog`
- `/designer-onboarding`
- `/terms`
- `/privacy`
- `/shipping`
- `/refund`
- `/cookie-policy`

## üéØ Logo Display in Google Search

To ensure your logo appears in Google search results when users search for "wedesign" or "wedesignz":

1. **Organization Schema**: ‚úÖ Implemented with logo URL
2. **Logo File**: ‚úÖ Located at `/public/Logos/WD LOGO2048BLACK.png`
3. **Additional Steps Required**:
   - Submit your site to Google Search Console
   - Verify ownership of your domain
   - Ensure the logo is accessible (publicly available)
   - The logo should be at least 112x112px (yours is 2048x2048px - perfect!)
   - Wait for Google to crawl and index your site (can take days to weeks)
